# Kanban Board Explanation

A Kanban board is a visual project management tool used to track tasks as they move through different stages of development. It uses columns such as "To Do", "In Progress", "Testing" and "Done" to represent the workflow of tasks.

In the ZakaWise project, the Kanban board visualizes the development process by clearly showing the status of each task. Each column represents a stage in the workflow, allowing easy tracking of progress.

The board helps limit work-in-progress (WIP) by ensuring that only a few tasks are worked on at a time. This prevents overload and improves focus, especially in a solo development environment.

Additionally, the Kanban board supports Agile principles such as continuous delivery and adaptability. Tasks can be updated, moved, or reprioritized easily, allowing the system to evolve based on development needs.

The inclusion of custom columns such as "Testing" and "Blocked" improves workflow visibility by highlighting quality assurance stages and identifying bottlenecks early.

Overall, the Kanban board ensures efficient task management, better organization, and improved productivity in the ZakaWise system.
